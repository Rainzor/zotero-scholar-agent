BERT: Pre-training of Deep Bidirectional Transformers for Language Understanding
Jacob Devlin, Ming-Wei Chang, Kenton Lee, Kristina Toutanova. NAACL 2019.

Abstract. We introduce BERT, which stands for Bidirectional Encoder
Representations from Transformers. Unlike recent language representation
models, BERT is designed to pre-train deep bidirectional representations from
unlabeled text by jointly conditioning on both left and right context in all
layers.

[page 2]
1 Introduction
BERT uses the Transformer encoder architecture (Vaswani et al., 2017) but,
unlike left-to-right language models, conditions on bidirectional context.
The pre-trained BERT model can be fine-tuned with just one additional output
layer to create state-of-the-art models for a wide range of tasks.

3 Pre-training BERT
We pre-train using two unsupervised tasks: the Masked Language Model (MLM),
which masks 15% of input tokens and predicts them, and Next Sentence
Prediction (NSP). Unlike the sinusoidal positional encodings of the original
Transformer, BERT uses learned positional embeddings.

5 Results
BERT obtains new state-of-the-art results on eleven natural language
processing tasks, including pushing the GLUE score to 80.5% and SQuAD v1.1
question answering Test F1 to 93.2.
