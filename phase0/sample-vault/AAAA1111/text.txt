Attention Is All You Need
Ashish Vaswani, Noam Shazeer, Niki Parmar, Jakob Uszkoreit, Llion Jones,
Aidan N. Gomez, Lukasz Kaiser, Illia Polosukhin. NeurIPS 2017.

Abstract. The dominant sequence transduction models are based on complex
recurrent or convolutional neural networks. We propose the Transformer, a
model architecture relying entirely on an attention mechanism, dispensing
with recurrence and convolutions entirely. Experiments show these models to
be superior in quality while being more parallelizable and requiring
significantly less time to train.

[page 2]
1 Introduction
Recurrent models factor computation along the symbol positions of the input
and output sequences, which precludes parallelization within training
examples. The Transformer avoids recurrence and instead relies entirely on
self-attention to compute representations of its input and output.

3 Model Architecture
The Transformer uses stacked self-attention and point-wise, fully connected
layers for both the encoder and decoder. We employ multi-head attention.
Scaled dot-product attention computes softmax(QK^T / sqrt(d_k)) V. The
scaling factor 1/sqrt(d_k) prevents the dot products from growing large in
magnitude, which would push the softmax into regions of small gradients.

3.5 Positional Encoding
Since the model contains no recurrence, we inject information about the
relative or absolute position of tokens using sine and cosine functions of
different frequencies. This lets the model attend to relative positions and
potentially extrapolate to sequence lengths longer than those seen in
training.

6 Results
On the WMT 2014 English-to-German translation task the big Transformer model
achieves 28.4 BLEU, and 41.8 BLEU on English-to-French, establishing a new
state of the art at a fraction of the training cost of previous models.
